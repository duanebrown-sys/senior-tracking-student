Financial Aid
FAFSA, award letters, and loan decisions
Deadline: May 30
This is the most critical step. Without completing financial aid, you may not be able to afford to enroll — even if you've been accepted. BJ: your deadline is May 30. Do not skip this.
  • 1
    Log into studentaid.gov
    Sign in with your FSA ID. If you forgot it, click "Forgot Username/Password" and reset using your email or phone.
  • 2
    Check your FAFSA was processed
    Under "My FAFSA," confirm the status says "Processed Successfully." Anything else — bring your laptop to your advisor immediately.
  • 3
    Find your financial aid award letter
    Log into your college's student portal. Look for a tab called "Financial Aid," "My Aid," or "Award Letter."
    Tip: Google "[your school name] student financial aid portal" if you're unsure where to find it.
  • 4
    Accept your aid offer
    Grants = free money, accept these. Work-study = optional on-campus job. Loans = money you repay, only accept what you need.
  • 5
    Complete loan entrance counseling if required
    If you accepted federal loans, most schools require a short online counseling session and a Master Promissory Note. Your portal will show if these are due.
  • 6
    Screenshot your completed award page
    Show this to your advisor so your checklist can be marked complete.
BJ — your financial aid deadline is May 30. That is 45 days away. Schedule time with your advisor this week to go through steps 1–5 together.

Admissions
Official acceptance and enrollment confirmation
Most complete
This confirms your seat is officially held. You need documentation to show it's done.
  • 1
    Find your official acceptance letter
    Log into the admissions portal (Common App or school-specific). Download or screenshot your acceptance letter.
  • 2
    Confirm your intent to enroll
    Most schools have a "Confirm Enrollment" button in your admissions portal. This is separate from paying the deposit.
  • 3
    Set up your school email
    Your college will send instructions. Do this now — all deadlines and account access go to that address from here on.
💳
Enrollment Deposit
Paying to secure your spot
Most complete
The enrollment deposit (usually $100–$500) locks in your spot. If you don't pay it, the school can give your seat away.
  • 1
    Find the deposit payment page
    Usually in your admissions portal. Look for "Enrollment Deposit," "Confirm Enrollment," or "Pay Deposit."
  • 2
    Pay using a debit card, credit card, or e-check
    If you don't have a card, ask a parent, guardian, or trusted adult to help. Some schools have payment plans for the deposit — call admissions and ask.
    Tip: Some schools will waive the deposit for hardship. Call admissions and ask directly — the worst they say is no.
  • 3
    Save your confirmation receipt
    Screenshot or print it and bring it to your advisor. This is your proof the deposit is done.

Logistics
Housing, transportation, and move-in planning
JayKyra: May 1
Where you sleep, how you get there, and what you eat on day one — these need to be locked in before school starts, not the week of.
  • 1
    Submit your housing application (if living on campus)
    Log into the student housing portal. Apply for a room and select your preferences (roommate, room type, dining plan).
    JayKyra: Dorms open May 1. Confirm your housing deposit is submitted before that date.
  • 2
    Confirm your move-in date and time
    Schools assign specific move-in windows. Check your email and housing portal for yours. Add it to your calendar.
  • 3
    Plan transportation to campus
    Figure out how you're getting there and the cost. Options: family car, bus/Greyhound, Amtrak. Budget for return trips during breaks.
  • 4
    If living at home — confirm your commute plan
    Map your daily route. Know your bus lines, drive time, or carpool options — and how early you need to leave for 8am classes.

Personal Supplies
Laptop, bedding, and day-one essentials
5 students incomplete
You don't need everything on day one — but you need the basics. A laptop is non-negotiable for most college programs. If cost is a barrier, there are options.
  • 1
    Confirm whether your school provides a laptop
    Some schools include a laptop or tech fee in tuition. Check your financial aid letter or your school's IT page.
  • 2
    If you need a laptop — flag it to your advisor now
    Rise Academy may have emergency funds or partner connections. Don't wait until August to say something.
  • 3
    Build a short list of what you need for move-in
    Dorms: sheets (Twin XL), towels, toiletries, power strip, hangers. Commuters: backpack, folders, charger, student ID.

Step 2 — Intake & assessment
Documents, CASAS test, and program fit review
After application
An Intake Coordinator will contact you to collect your documents and schedule your CASAS assessment. This is how they figure out which program path fits you best.
  • 1
    Gather your documents ahead of time
    Have ready: a photo ID (driver's license or state ID), your Social Security card, and proof of income or household status if requested. Having these ready speeds up the process.
  • 2
    Schedule and complete your CASAS assessment
    CASAS is a basic skills test (reading and math). It's not a pass/fail — it helps the program understand where you are and what support you need. You cannot fail this test.
    Tip: It's okay if you feel nervous. The CASAS is used to help you, not judge you. Just do your best.
  • 3
    Meet with the Intake Coordinator for your fit review
    They'll look at your application, documents, and CASAS score to confirm you're a good match for the program and discuss which path makes sense for you.

Step 3 — Director approval & enrollment
Final review and official program start
Final step
Once the Director approves your enrollment, you're in — and the benefits start. This includes paid work experience, free training, and supportive services.

    The Intake Coordinator submits your file to the Director for final approval. You'll be notified by phone or email — make sure you're checking both.

    Choose your program path
    Once enrolled, you'll select your track. Options include GED completion, Occupational Skills Training, or Work Experience ($18/hr). You may be able to combine paths.

    Show up to your first session
    Training is available in person or virtual. Your coordinator will send you the schedule. Showing up consistently is what earns your incentives and opens doors to employment.
